A Fighter Forged by Adversity

Justin Kasieta knows what it means to fight for survival—and to win against impossible odds.

Born and raised in Michigan's Upper Peninsula, Justin's life changed forever when his father died of cancer when he was just 15 years old. At 13, he became the caretaker for his four younger siblings.

When the family entered Michigan's foster care system, Justin experienced firsthand the failures of government bureaucracy. Four foster homes. Four different high schools. And the state took over $18,000 in Social Security survivor benefits meant for his family.

Instead of giving up, Justin fought back. He testified before the Michigan Legislature for Senate Bill 18, interned in both the Michigan state legislature and U.S. Congress, and graduated from the University of Michigan.

His advocacy has been featured on NPR, CBS News, MSN, and GPB News. His work helped reform foster care systems in 15 states, and the Trump administration issued warnings to states about this practice affecting over 25,000 foster children and $174 million annually.

Now, Justin is ready to take that same fighting spirit to Washington—to stand up for Michigan families, protect our children, and restore common sense to our government.
